g_2007 <- gapminder %>%
  filter(year == 2007)

p1 <- ggplot(g_2007, aes(x = gdpPercap, y = lifeExp, color = continent, size = pop)) +
  geom_point(alpha = 0.7) +
  scale_color_viridis(discrete = TRUE, option = "D") +
  scale_x_log10() + 
  labs(x = "GDP per Capita (log scale)", y = "Life Expectancy", title = "Life Expectancy vs GDP per Capita (2007)",color = "Continent", size = "Population") 

i_p1 <- ggplotly(p1)

i_p1

The scatter plot shows the relationship between life expectancy and GDP per capita, with GDP per capita displayed on a log scale along the x-axis and life expectancy on the y-axis. Each point represents a country, and the color of the points indicates the continent the country belongs to. Key observations include a positive correlation: as GDP per capita increases, life expectancy generally rises. European countries tend to cluster in the higher GDP and life expectancy range, while many African countries have lower values for both. Additionally, there are some outliers where countries with relatively low GDP per capita still achieve higher life expectancy. This likely reflects differences in economic development, healthcare access, and public health policies across regions.
